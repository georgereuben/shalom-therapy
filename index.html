Meet Our Therapists

Manju George

Counselling Psychologist

Manju is a counselling psychologist with expertise in guiding individuals through emotional and personal growth challenges. At Shalom Therapy, she believes that true healing comes from understanding the whole person, not just treating symptoms.

With advanced degrees in Counselling and Developmental Psychology from the University of Kent, UK and certifications in Person-Centered Psychotherapy and Special Needs Education, she offers a compassionate, client-focused approach that empowers you to discover your own solutions.

Based out of Kochi, she provides a safe, supportive environment where you can explore your thoughts and feelings without judgment, developing the insights and skills needed to overcome challenges and live a more fulfilling life. Her online therapy sessions are available to clients throughout India.

Manushi Parwani

Counselling Psychologist

Manushi is a counselling psychologist and life-skills trainer with expertise in providing safe spaces for emotional exploration and growth. With an M.Sc. in Counselling Psychology from CHRIST (Deemed to be) University, Delhi-NCR, and certification as a Person-Centered Therapist, she has trained under Meenakshi Kirtane (Director Psychodrama Institute of India and Maanas- The Inside Story).

Her therapeutic approach is deeply-rooted in the Rogerian belief of humanistic approach and empathy. She uses an eclectic approach in her sessions, incorporating Person-centered Therapy and Solution-focused approach at its core. She has worked with clients ranging from 9 years old to 45 years old, with particular expertise in substance use issues, relationship issues, and anger management.

If you are looking for a safe space to share and explore your thoughts, feelings and emotions, Manushi provides compassionate, client-focused support to help you navigate life's challenges.

Personalized therapy sessions using a person-centered approach that respects your unique perspective and empowers you to discover your own solutions for mental health and personal growth.

For Families Abroad & Children with Special Needs

While completing her degree at the University of Kent, Manju worked as a substitute teacher in schools across Kent and London — and many of her students were NRI and international children facing the very same adjustments she was navigating as a student herself. The cultural tightrope, the longing for familiarity, the quiet work of belonging somewhere new — she knew it from both sides of the classroom. That’s the understanding she brings to every young person she works with.

For children with special needs, her certification in Special Needs Education ensures every session is thoughtfully adapted. Pacing, communication style, and activities are shaped around each child’s strengths and individual requirements. Every young person deserves support that truly meets them where they are.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer online counselling and therapy in Kochi?

Absolutely. While Shalom Therapy is based in Kochi, every session is conducted securely online — so where you live makes no difference. We work with clients right across India and around the world, including Indian families living abroad. Wherever you are, all you need is a private space and an internet connection.

What issues can counselling help with?

We support adults and young people with anxiety, stress, low self-esteem, relationship difficulties, grief, personal growth, and substance-use and anger-related concerns. We also offer life skills training for children and teenagers and specialised support for children with special needs.

How much does a counselling session cost?

We offer a free initial consultation so you can decide if it feels right before committing. Session fees are kept affordable and are shared when you book. You can book a free discovery call online to discuss your needs and pricing.

Is what I share in therapy confidential?

Yes. Sessions are private and confidential. What you share stays between you and your therapist, within the standard ethical and legal limits of professional counselling practice.

Do you offer life skills training for teenagers living abroad (NRI families)?

Yes. We run interactive online life skills programmes designed especially for Indian children and teenagers living abroad, helping them with cultural adaptation, emotional regulation, communication, confidence, and digital safety. Having lived and worked with NRI students abroad, Manju brings first-hand understanding to these sessions.

How do I book a session?

You can schedule online through our booking link, or send a message via the contact form below. We'll get back to you to confirm a time that works for you.
